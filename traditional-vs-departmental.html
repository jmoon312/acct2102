Step 1

Plantwide rate

one rate for the whole shop

Peach Lab Coffee is a small Atlanta roaster. The plant runs two products through two departments:

Roasting — the machine-heavy department where green beans are roasted and (for cold brew) cold-extracted.
Packaging — the labor-heavy department where coffee is portioned, bagged or kegged, and labeled.

Annual production plan: 1,000 batches of Cold Brew Concentrate (sold in growlers to local cafes) and 1,000 batches of Whole Bean (sold in 5–lb bags to grocery stores).

Total budgeted manufacturing overhead is $120,000 for the year. Roasting consumes 4,000 machine hours. The cost team's first pass uses a single plantwide rate, applied based on machine hours.

Step 4

Which method tells the truth?

intuition first, then the dollars

The plantwide rate spreads all $120,000 of overhead based on machine hours alone — even though $20,000 of that is packaging cost driven by labor hours, not machines. Departmental rates fix this.
