Step 2

Markup on full manufacturing cost

a price built up from the manufactured-cost base

Most product-cost systems start with full manufacturing cost — direct materials, direct labor, variable mfg overhead, and an allocated share of fixed mfg overhead. Selling and admin costs (plus the profit target) get layered on through the markup.

Allocated fixed mfg overhead per bag at 50,000 bags = $80,000 ÷ 50,000 = $1.60.

Step 5

The market pushes back — flip the problem

when customers won't pay your cost-based price

You bring the $8.80 price to a roundtable of wholesale café buyers. They tell you the going rate for a comparable 12-oz private-label specialty bag tops out at $7.50. Cost-based pricing told you what to charge; the market is telling you what you can charge.

Management still expects the same $60,000 profit at the same planned 50,000-bag volume.
