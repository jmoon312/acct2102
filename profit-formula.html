Step 1

The sneaker company

predict profit at a given volume

A sneaker company sells running shoes for $50 per pair. Fixed costs total $500 per month (rent, insurance). Variable costs are $10 per pair sold (materials, packaging).

Profit = (Selling price × Unit sales) − (Fixed costs + Variable cost per unit × Unit sales)
In other words: Revenue − Total Variable Costs − Total Fixed Costs = Profit

If the company sells 100 pairs this month, fill in each piece of the formula:

Revenue Total Variable Cost Total Fixed Cost Profit
