Step 3

Which cost driver?

five scenarios

The choice of cost driver shapes how overhead lands on individual products. For each scenario below, decide which driver best fits the production process described.

Scenario 1 — Automated stitching line
Coach is installing a new automated stitching system for its bestselling tote line. The machines run continuously during production with minimal operator involvement.
Scenario 2 — Hand-finished luxury collection
Coach's premium Heritage collection requires master artisans to hand-stitch and burnish each bag. Each bag takes 6–8 hours of skilled labor.
Scenario 3 — Mixed production facility
Coach's crossbody bag line uses automated cutting and a semi-automated sewing process, but each bag still requires 2 hours of hand-finishing and quality inspection.
Scenario 4 — New robotic leather cutting
Coach just invested $2M in robotic leather-cutting equipment for all product lines. Operators monitor the machines but rarely intervene.
Scenario 5 — Custom monogramming department
Coach's monogramming service is entirely manual — skilled workers use hand tools to personalize bags to customer specifications, one at a time.
