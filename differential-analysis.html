Before she can schedule the meeting, a purchasing manager from TechRetailer calls with a one-time offer: 1,000 ProConnect adapters at $15 per unit, well below the regular $20. TechRetailer handles its own distribution, so HP wouldn't incur any variable selling and administrative costs on this order. HP's production team says the ProConnect line can produce up to 5,000 units per month and would need a temporary quality inspector at $500 per month to handle the extra volume.

The VP now faces a combined question: should she shut down ProConnect, or keep it running and take the special order? The equipment used by the division was purchased last year for $100,000 and is being depreciated at $4,000 per month.
