Step 2

Make or buy?

pure outsourcing analysis

A contract manufacturer offers to supply all 2,000 standard adapters HP needs each month for $11 per unit. If HP outsources, it can eliminate $3,000 per month in dedicated supervision and tooling costs tied to the standard adapter line. Variable selling & administrative costs would remain unchanged — HP still handles distribution either way.

Evaluate the outsourcing offer on a pure cost basis — ignore capacity effects for now.

What is the relevant per-unit cost of making each standard adapter? Only costs that go away if HP stops making them.
What is the net effect on monthly profit from outsourcing? Positive = outsourcing increases profit; negative = decreases it.
Based on cost alone, should HP outsource the standard adapter?
Step 3

What about capacity?

the opportunity cost changes everything

HP's sales team reports unfilled demand: 300 corporate clients are requesting premium hubs each month, but HP has had to turn them away because the assembly line is fully booked. If HP outsources the standard adapter, those machine hours become available for hub production.

How many machine hours does outsourcing the standard adapter free up?
How many additional premium hubs will HP produce and sell? Consider both freed capacity and market demand.
What additional contribution margin do those extra hubs generate?
Now combine the outsourcing cost from Step 2 with the capacity opportunity. What is the combined net effect on monthly profit? Outsourcing effect + additional hub CM.
With capacity in the picture, should HP outsource the standard adapter?
